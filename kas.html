Kasargod

AREA:1992 sq. km

The northernmost district of Kerala, Kasaragod is renowned for its hills, forts and water bodies. The coir and handloom industry here are extremely famous around the world. The place is also popular for its ancient temples and rich biodiversity. It is an important border district as well that regulates trade with a lot of neighbouring States. Legends regarding the district greet you at every turn on a visit here. A trip here is incomplete without visiting Bekal Fort, the most majestic and well maintained fort in South India.

The town of Kasargod experiences temperate climate conditions owing to the proximity to the sea. The summers are hot and humid while winters are cool and pleasant. The temperature during winters varies from 12 to 20 degrees Celcius and thus serves as just the ideal time to explore the town. It is, hence, best to visit the town between the months of September and January. Monsoon season receives heavy rainfall and thus is not recommended as a right time to visit the place.

The top attractions to visit in Kasaragod are: Bekal Fort, Parappa Wildlife Sanctuary, Ranipuram Hills, Thaikadappuram Beach, Malom Wildlife Sanctuary, Cheruvathur, Ananthapura Lake Temple, Mallikarjuna Temple, Hosdurg fort.
